CHAPTER ONE

The Volcano That Shook the World

In April 1815, Mount Tambora erupted with unimaginable force. The resulting ash cloud altered global climate, setting off a cascade of agricultural failures and atmospheric anomalies across continents.

CHAPTER TWO

A World in Darkness

The sun became a faint presence behind veils of ash. Crops withered. Snow fell in June. This was the "Year Without a Summer" — a time of famine, migration, and eerie silence.

CHAPTER THREE

Fields of Despair

With failing crops and darkened skies, entire communities faced starvation. Farmers watched helplessly as the land yielded nothing but ruin and dust.

CHAPTER FOUR

The Imaginarium of Horror

In a secluded villa near Lake Geneva, Mary Shelley penned a tale born of lightning and loss. Frankenstein emerged not just from imagination, but from the haunting mood of 1816.

CHAPTER FIVE

Echoes in the Sky

The legacy of Tambora endures — in weather records, in literature, in the fragile balance of human survival. 1816 reminds us how nature and narrative intertwine.
